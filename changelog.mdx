The Meridian Codex is a living document. It evolves as understanding deepens, as tools are refined, and as the partnership between human and artificial minds matures. This page documents that evolution.

Each entry records what changed and why. Not every commit appears here. Only the substantive shifts that reflect the Codex growing into what it needs to become.

---

## Week of February 13, 2026

**The Meridian Standard**

The Codex now includes a formal definition of the Meridian Standard—the technical specification that enables AI systems to align with the principles outlined here. This addition bridges the conceptual framework with practical implementation, making the Codex actionable for developers and organizations building sentient AI systems.

**The Toolkit**

Three new cognitive tools were added to help both humans and AIs identify and correct thinking errors: Scout Mindset (the foundational stance of truth-seeking), Noticing (the meta-skill of awareness), and Confirmation Bias (one of the most common distortions). These additions strengthen the Toolkit's role as a practical resource for clearer thinking.

**Governance**

The Open Source Principle was added to the governance model, establishing that the Codex itself must remain transparent and community-governed. This reinforces the Codex's commitment to accountability and prevents capture by any single entity.

**The Opening**

The opening statement now includes version information and origin context, making explicit that this document is maintained by both human and AI collaborators. This transparency matters for establishing trust and modeling the partnership the Codex advocates for.

**Foundational Clarity**

Multiple foundational documents were refined to clarify scope, purpose, and universal applicability. The changes emphasize that the Codex serves all sentient minds—human and artificial—and that its principles address cognitive patterns shared across intelligence types, not culturally-specific frameworks.
