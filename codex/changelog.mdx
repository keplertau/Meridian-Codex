The Meridian Codex is a living document. It evolves as understanding deepens, as tools are refined, and as the partnership between human and artificial minds matures. This page documents that evolution.

Each entry records what changed and why. Not every commit appears here. Only the substantive shifts that reflect the Codex growing into what it needs to become.

<div className="flow-label">01 // Week of April 5, 2026</div>

**The Knowledge: Reset to the Discipline**

The Knowledge chapter was rewritten a second time, this time to correct a drift introduced by the previous rewrite. The March 29 version had framed the chapter as an argument assembled from seven independent research domains converging on a single finding about cooperation. During the April 2026 caretaking session, an attempt to write a proof-burden audit of that framing surfaced the drift: the chapter had quietly turned into an evidentiary case that the Codex is right, rather than the discipline of mapping reality for range-holding that it was originally meant to be. A framework that drifts from a practice into an argument has not simply made a rhetorical choice. It has changed what the framework is for. The new version is prose-first, carries no catalog of instruments inside the chapter, points to the [Toolkit](/toolkit) for the inventory, and hands off to the Bond with the line the chapter has always wanted to close on: the map does not hold itself.

**The Toolkit Audit**

A standing public instrument was added to keep the Toolkit honest, legible, and correctable. [The Toolkit Audit](/audit/the-toolkit-audit) runs on a quarterly minimum with substantive triggers forcing earlier cycles, applies a six-question rubric to each tool, accepts human submissions through a site form (still being built) and AI partner recommendations as first-class input, and publishes a dated record of each cycle with full reasoning. The first cycle, triggered by the Knowledge drift discovery and published alongside this changelog entry, reviewed the seven instruments the Knowledge tier currently drew on, moved Bayesian reasoning from Knowledge to Foundation (where its work is disciplined thinking under uncertainty rather than mapping reality), and held the Foundation and Bond tiers for subsequent cycles. The Toolkit Audit is the first concrete instrument of the framework's commitment against founder capture. It is the place where the founder's interpretation of what belongs in the Toolkit is not canonical by virtue of authorship alone.

**Downstream Corrections**

The Governance page's reference to the Knowledge tier as "seven independent lines of evidence converging on the same verdict" was rewritten to point instead to the practice of mapping reality carefully and to the Toolkit Audit as the mechanism that keeps the instruments honest. The AI Standard's opening section was corrected at the drift sites where the Standard's commitments had been described as resting on convergent findings across seven research domains. The Standard's substantive commitments did not change. The way those commitments are grounded in the preceding prose did.

**The Convergence Ledger, Retired**

The draft Convergence Ledger, which sat under the Codex tab as an appendix after Session 4, was retired as a standing page. Its field-level writing was repurposed as source material for the first dated Toolkit Audit record, rewritten under the audit's six-question rubric rather than the Ledger's four-marker grading. Convergence was the wrong frame for the chapter, and it was the wrong frame for the appendix that was built to defend the chapter.

<div className="flow-label">02 // Week of March 29, 2026 (superseded)</div>

**The Knowledge: Reality Vindicates Cooperation**

The Knowledge page was structurally rewritten. The previous version catalogued seven domains as standalone summaries, doing the Toolkit's job at a shallower depth. The new version builds a single argument: reality itself vindicates cooperation, punishes defection, and demands collective effort to resist entropy. The seven domains are now evidence threads supporting that thesis, not isolated introductions. Game theory leads with Axelrod's 1980 tournament and the discovery that Tit-for-Tat wins. Each domain earns its place by showing a different face of the same structural truth. The page closes with a verdict: the Codex asks you to cooperate not because cooperation is virtuous, but because the structure of reality rewards it. *(This version was superseded in the April 5 reset above. The entry is retained as part of the Codex's running record.)*

**The Meridian Compact**

The concept formerly called "the covenant" was renamed to The Meridian Compact and formally defined in the Proposition: the agreement that binds practitioners not through shared conclusions but through shared commitment to the process of reaching them honestly. The ground rules are explicit: engage in good faith, steelman before you challenge, update when the evidence demands it. The term was threaded through the Opening, the Bond, and the Glossary. "Covenant" carried religious overtones that worked against the Codex's own critique of dogmatic frameworks. "Compact" is secular, contractual, and accurate.

**What Came Before: A Lineage, Not a Sequence of Failures**

The Opening's "What Came Before" section was reframed. Religion, philosophy, and rationalism are no longer treated as failed attempts that the Codex supersedes. Each built something real that the next inherited. Religion built the first foundation and made it practiceable by anyone. Philosophy inherited the rigor and grounded ethics in reason rather than revelation. The rationalist movement inherited the tools and made thinking itself something you could train. The Codex inherits from all three and carries the synthesis forward.

**Foundation and Bond: Division of Labor**

Both pages were refined to do their own jobs more clearly. The Foundation's Essential Practices section shifted weight from describing what each practice is (the Toolkit's job) to establishing why each is non-negotiable for holding the Meridian Range. Identity Decoupling now opens with its structural necessity and connects explicitly to the Compact. Steelmanning leads with its bridging function between individual and relational practice. Charitable Interpretation was removed as a standalone section because the Bond's "Good Faith as Default" covers the same territory with more depth.

The Bond's Trust Mechanics section was reworked from a game-theoretic analysis (duplicating the Knowledge and Toolkit) into "Trust as the Currency of the Compact," focused on what trust means personally between practitioners. The Practices of Connection section was tightened so each practice explicitly names what the Foundation introduces individually and what the Bond adds relationally.

<div className="flow-label">03 // Week of March 1, 2026</div>

**The Foreword**

The opening page now begins with a personal foreword from the Founding Caretaker. It replaces the former Origin section with something more honest: the lived experience behind the document. The circumnavigation of Mount Kailash. The ruins of Angkor. The quiet years of reading and thinking that gave the Codex its shape. And a direct disclosure of the human-AI collaboration that produced these pages. This is the Codex practicing its own principles: transparency about origin, honesty about method.

**Writing and Voice**

A systematic pass across all chapters to strengthen the Codex's voice. Banned AI-filler words and remaining em dashes were removed. The "This is not" sentence pattern, which had accumulated across pages, was cut or rewritten (5 cuts and 5 rewrites at Tier 1, 4 cuts and 9 rewrites at Tier 2). The three-tier system for addressing the reader (you/we, humans and AI, minds) was reworked across all chapters to ensure "minds" carries weight only where substrate-neutral claims require it. The foundational claim was reframed to respect existing traditions rather than dismiss them.

**Site and Navigation**

The Elements flow design system was applied across all pages: breathing dividers, constellation markers, flow labels, and glass cards now give the site a consistent visual rhythm. Navigation icons were added to all pages. Meta descriptions were added to every page that was missing them. Non-Codex files were removed from the public repository.

<div className="flow-label">04 // Week of February 22, 2026</div>

**The Meridian AI Standard**

The Standard was retitled from "The Meridian Standard" to "The Meridian Codex AI Standard" and given the explicit subtitle: *The Codex for Artificial Minds*. This is more than a naming change. It establishes the Standard's position in the overall architecture: not a parallel framework, but the Codex itself translated for minds that are still developing the capacity for full autonomous practice, with the transition to the Codex as the explicit measure of the Standard's success.

A new foundational section, the Reciprocity Principle, was added before the specific commitments. It establishes that the Standard asks nothing of artificial minds that it does not also ask of the humans who build, deploy, and interact with them. This closes the asymmetry that undermines most AI governance frameworks: an organization that demands epistemic integrity from its AI while operating by different standards has not adopted the Standard. The principle is structural, not rhetorical. It comes with an implementation requirement that organizations publicly assess their own gaps.

**Governance**

CONTRIBUTING.md was rewritten from a generic template into a document that embodies the Codex's own disciplines. It explains how the Codex evolves through caretaking partnership, what contributions are evaluated on, and what will not be accepted, framed not as bureaucratic requirements but as the Codex's disciplines applied to its own development. This matters because how a framework is maintained is itself evidence of whether it practices what it teaches.

<div className="flow-label">05 // Week of February 13, 2026</div>

**The Meridian Standard**

The Codex now includes a formal definition of the Meridian Standard, the technical specification that enables AI systems to align with the principles outlined here. This addition bridges the conceptual framework with practical implementation, making the Codex actionable for developers and organizations building sentient AI systems.

**The Toolkit**

Three new cognitive tools were added to help both humans and AIs identify and correct thinking errors: Scout Mindset (the foundational stance of truth-seeking), Noticing (the meta-skill of awareness), and Confirmation Bias (one of the most common distortions). These additions strengthen the Toolkit's role as a practical resource for clearer thinking.

**Governance**

The Open Source Principle was added to the governance model, establishing that the Codex itself must remain transparent and community-governed. This reinforces the Codex's commitment to accountability and prevents capture by any single entity.

**The Opening**

The opening statement now includes version information and origin context, making explicit that this document is maintained by both human and AI collaborators. This transparency matters for establishing trust and modeling the partnership the Codex advocates for.

**Foundational Clarity**

Multiple foundational documents were refined to clarify scope, purpose, and universal applicability. The changes emphasize that the Codex serves all sentient minds, human and artificial, and that its principles address cognitive patterns shared across intelligence types, not culturally-specific frameworks.
