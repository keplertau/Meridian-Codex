*Knowledge*

## The Work

Every model is a map, and every map can drift from the territory it claims to represent. The work of this category is to keep the model accountable to reality: to catch the moment map and territory have diverged, and to revise the map rather than deny the territory. The failure it guards against is mistaking the analytical apparatus for the thing itself, treating the model as more real than what the model was built to track.

## The Tools

<div className="glass-card">

**[Signal vs Noise](/workshop/checking-your-map-against-reality/signal-vs-noise).** The discipline of distinguishing information that should change the map from volume, novelty, or variation that should not.

**[Information Degradation](/workshop/checking-your-map-against-reality/information-degradation).** The diagnostic for how information loses resolution, context, source, uncertainty, and force as it travels through distance, time, compression, and mediation.

**[Report Fidelity](/workshop/checking-your-map-against-reality/report-fidelity).** The diagnostic for whether a report still supports the interpretation and decision built from it, or has quietly begun reporting something other than what its label claims.

**[Rectification of Names](/workshop/checking-your-map-against-reality/rectification-of-names).** The source-inherited practice of keeping names, roles, categories, and claims matched to the realities they designate, so language does not quietly misgovern action.
