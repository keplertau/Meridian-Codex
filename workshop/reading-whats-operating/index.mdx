*Knowledge*

## The Work

The work is to read what is operative in any system: what is actually generating its dynamics, what makes it stable or unstable, where it sits between Control and Decay. The reading runs cross-scale (individual thinking, relationship, organization, movement, civilization) and across temporal horizons (within-moment dynamics, lifetime, generational arc, civilizational time).

## The Tools

<div className="glass-card">

**[Entropy](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/entropy).** Systems degrade without active maintenance; the lens for how order requires sustained energy to hold against decay.

**[Prisoner's Dilemma](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/prisoners-dilemma).** Situations where individually rational defection produces a worse shared outcome than cooperation would have produced.

**[Feedback Loops](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/feedback-loops).** How a system's outputs return as inputs, either amplifying the pattern or stabilizing it.

**[Network Effects](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/network-effects).** How the value, cost, or danger of a choice changes as more actors make the same choice.

**[Emergence](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/emergence).** System-level properties that arise from interactions among parts and cannot be understood by inspecting the parts alone.

**[Leverage Points](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/leverage-points).** Where force sits inside the system before intervention begins.

**[Moloch](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/moloch).** Competitive race dynamics that reward actors for sacrificing shared values until everyone is worse off and no one can safely stop alone.

**[Inadequate Equilibria](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/inadequate-equilibria).** Bad stable states where visible improvements remain unused because no actor has enough incentive, authority, coordination, or permission to fix the system alone.

**[Goodhart's Law](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/goodharts-law).** How a useful measure degrades when people are pressured to optimize it as a target.

**[Legibility](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/legibility).** How institutions simplify reality so it can be seen, governed, counted, and controlled.

**[Tragedy of the Commons](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/tragedy-of-the-commons).** Shared-resource failure where private extraction is rewarded and depletion costs spread across the group and the future.

**[Mechanism Design](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/mechanism-design).** The diagnostic for reading what behavior a rule, incentive structure, or institutional process makes rational.

**[Schelling Points](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/schelling-points).** Tacit coordination around a salient point people expect others to expect, especially when communication is limited or incomplete.

**[Rules-in-Use](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/rules-in-use).** The diagnostic for what rule actually governs behavior here, and whether the formal rule constrains action when following it costs something.

**[Antifragility](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/antifragility).** Whether a system is harmed, preserved, or strengthened by volatility, stress, error, and disorder.

**[Lindy Effect](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/lindy-effect).** Survival over time as evidence for nonperishable things, without confusing endurance for truth or moral worth.

**[Red Queen Effect](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/red-queen-effect).** Adaptive pressure where continuous movement is required simply to maintain position because the surrounding field is moving too.

**[Chilling Effects](/workshop/reading-whats-operating/chilling-effects).** How anticipated punishment, surveillance, ambiguity, and social cost suppress behavior before punishment is applied.

</div>
