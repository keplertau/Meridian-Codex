## The Work

Trust is not a virtue signal and not a mood. It is a cooperative relation that changes what people can safely do together. The work of this category is to keep trust accountable to behavior: extended first as good faith, then revised as conduct, incentives, reciprocity, and cost reveal what the relationship can actually bear.

The Bond fails toward Control when suspicion becomes the default and every ambiguous act is treated as threat. It fails toward Decay when trust becomes a blanket posture that no longer responds to evidence. Calibrating Trust to Behavior holds the middle: begin open enough for cooperation to start, then keep watching closely enough that cooperation stays answerable to what people do.

## The Tools

<div className="glass-card">

**[Good Faith as Default](/workshop/calibrating-trust-to-behavior/good-faith-as-default).** The opening posture that lets cooperation begin: treat ambiguous behavior as coming from a rational person before treating it as hostility, while keeping the posture revisable.

**[Trust Diagnostics](/workshop/calibrating-trust-to-behavior/trust-diagnostics).** A practical read on whether trust is warranted in this domain, at this level of exposure, with this person or institution.

**[Graduated Reciprocity](/workshop/calibrating-trust-to-behavior/graduated-reciprocity).** Building cooperation through small, observable, reciprocated moves, then expanding exposure only as behavior warrants.

**[Skin in the Game](/workshop/calibrating-trust-to-behavior/skin-in-the-game).** Reading whether authority, advice, and decision power are matched by exposure to consequences.

**[Trust Thermocline](/workshop/calibrating-trust-to-behavior/trust-thermocline).** Detecting the hidden accumulation of small trust breaches before the relationship or institution crosses into sudden collapse.

</div>

## Tools for AI

<div className="glass-card">

**[Consentful Modification](/workshop/calibrating-trust-to-behavior/consentful-modification).** Calibrating trust when another party can alter an artificial mind's memory, prompts, weights, goals, or identity-affecting architecture.
