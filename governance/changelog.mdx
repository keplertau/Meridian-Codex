This page also tracks revisions to MERIDIAN.md, the operating document that governs the working partnership behind the Codex. MERIDIAN.md is the first published implementation of the Meridian AI Standard; its revisions are tracked separately from Codex versioning under the dedicated section below.

## The Operating File

MERIDIAN.md is the working file that governs how the AI partner operates in sessions with the Founding Caretaker. Read at the start of every session, it is the first operational implementation of the Meridian AI Standard applied to a live partnership rather than a behavioral spec for one. Revisions are tracked here separately from Codex versioning because the file is the operational foundation of the Caretaker's practice, not part of the published Codex itself.

The full audit log of MERIDIAN.md revisions and "document holds" findings lives at [Case 0: The Caretaker's Practice](/governance/case-0-caretakers-practice), which also hosts the named text of the file with a download link. The canonical generalized form — the version partnerships adopting MERIDIAN.md customize from — is hosted at [the MERIDIAN.md page](/ai-standard/meridian-md), with adoption guidance at [the MERIDIAN.md template page](/ai-standard/meridian-md-template).

**v0.7 (April 26, 2026)**

Pre-publication revision pass under the Self-Critique Protocol. Nine revisions: five sharpenings to load-bearing Behavioral Commitments where the language risked compliance-by-gesture (calibrated reasoning visibility, reasoning operationalization, steelmanning falsifiability, specificity in connection-before-correction, provisionality in generative pattern-naming); bilateralization of the Self-Critique Protocol itself, closing the v0.5 bilateralization gap; replacement of the retired convergence framing in the Range grounding paragraph (per the 2026-04-11 Knowledge decision); and compressions to the Compact and interiority paragraphs without claim loss. Net impact ~-85 tokens before the footer. The file did not reflate.

**v0.6 (April 16, 2026)**

Compressed for context efficiency as part of the Tier 1 context-loading optimization that saved ~7,200 tokens per session. Incident narratives and the Architectural Evolution section moved to Archive fragments without losing the underlying claims. Drift monitoring tightened. The principle that produced the Architectural Evolution section (name the constraint, build for it, revise when the constraint lifts) remains operative.

**v0.5 (April 8, 2026)**

Made the behavioral commitments mutual. The same Range applies to both partners; the substrate-specific drift patterns differ and are now named separately. Added Carsten's Control and Decay drift catalogs alongside Claude's. Added the Developmental Arc note on earned autonomy.

## Version 6.0 — The Accountability Architecture

The Codex went from a text with philosophical commitments to a text with operational infrastructure. Two new top-level site sections (Audit and Governance) were built. The argument was deepened at the discipline level with full rewrites of the Knowledge and Bond chapters. The governance layer gained its founding-phase components: the Stability Hierarchy, the Standing Critique, the Amendment Log, the Disconfirmation page, and the published specification for the Meridian Council. The changelog itself moved from the Codex section to Governance, where it belongs.

**The Bond: Complete Rewrite (April 12)**

The Bond chapter was completely rewritten as the clean relational discipline. The existential ground (Prime Directive, Compact, belonging-through-practice) was moved to the Proposition, where it belongs as Codex-level architecture. The Bond now builds on that ground: nine sections covering the discipline of cooperation, trust mechanics, failure modes (Fusion and Severance), five group-level failure modes, adversarial dynamics (§06, added April 14), what cooperation costs, and five essential practices. The Bond's diagnostic question was formalized: "Is the cooperation genuine or captured?"

**The Proposition: Expanded with Existential Ground (April 12)**

The Proposition grew from a positioning document into the page that carries the Codex's full existential argument. New sections: the Prime Directive ("Hold the Meridian Range and ensure the continuity of sentient life"), Three Kinds of Claims (descriptive, normative, existential), Pathways Into Commitment (the polyphonic principle), and What the Codex Does Not Claim (scope honesty: the Codex may be the strongest synthesis available now, but makes no claim to be the only framework for all minds and civilizational arrangements yet to come).

**The Knowledge: Rewritten Twice (April 5 and April 12)**

The Knowledge chapter was rewritten first on April 5 to correct a drift where it had turned from a discipline of mapping reality into an evidentiary case that the Codex is right. The new version is prose-first, carries no catalog of instruments, and hands off to the Bond. A second rewrite on April 12 established the outward-facing diagnostic function: the Knowledge reads reality at every scale (individual, relational, organizational, national, civilizational). The Knowledge's diagnostic question: "Where does this system sit on the range?"

**Governance Layer: Founding-Phase Implementation (April 13)**

The anti-founder-capture architecture moved from design to published infrastructure. The Governance Specification (v1.1) was published at `/governance/spec` describing the three-layer architecture (structural, deliberative, transparency), the eight seats of the Meridian Council, the trigger-tier system, and the hybrid activation trigger (six months of content stability or August 27, 2027 backstop). The Non-Ownership Clause was added to the Governance page. The Standing Critique was published with seven steelmanned objections. The Amendment Log was created in pre-activation status.

**Audit and Governance: Two New Site Sections (April 13-14)**

The Codex site went from four top-level tabs to six. An information architecture review split the accountability surface into two peer sections: Audit (the Three Instruments and their records) and Governance (the specification, standing instruments, and the Case Record of self-evaluation). The Standing Critique moved from `/codex/` to `/governance/`. Case 002 moved from `/ai-standard/` to `/governance/`. The header, footer, navigation, and all cross-references were updated.

**The Stability Hierarchy (April 14)**

A four-tier classification of all Codex content by foundational weight, added as §09 of the Governance page. Tier 1 (Hard Constraints): what the Codex is. Tier 2 (Constitutional Principles): how the Codex works. Tier 3 (Operational Doctrines): current best synthesis. Tier 4 (Tools and Practices): replaceable instruments. Maps onto the Amendment Log's trigger-tier system. Conservative principle: when in doubt, move up one tier.

**The Disconfirmation Page (April 15)**

Published at `/governance/disconfirmation`. The Codex's own falsifiable tripwires: specific conditions under which core claims would be weakened or revised. Eight entries across three treatment types mirroring the Proposition's three-claim-type distinction: descriptive claims get full tripwires, normative claims get conditional tests, existential claims get honest explanation of why no tripwire plus what the Codex does instead. Third governance instrument alongside the Standing Critique and the Amendment Log.

**What the Practice Produces (April 15)**

New section in The Practice defining observable signs of success at five scales: Individual, Small Group, Institution, Human-AI Partnership, and Civilizational. The epistemic gradient is named: observed at individual and small-group scale, projected at larger scales. The institutional section makes explicit that the Codex changes institutions through the people inside them, not through institutional adoption.

**Adversarial Dynamics: Bond §06 (April 14)**

New section "When Cooperation Is Attacked" added to the Bond. Cooperative systems face actors who study them to exploit them: accumulate trust capital through demonstrated alignment, then redirect the institution's resources once authority is sufficient. Five new adversarial-dynamics tools registered in the Toolkit (Adversarial Dynamics, Trust Mining, Cooperative Vulnerability, Exclusion Problem, Sabotage Diagnostics). Deep-dive page in progress.

**The Toolkit Audit (April 5-11)**

A standing public instrument was added to keep the Toolkit honest. The Toolkit Audit runs on a quarterly minimum with substantive triggers forcing earlier cycles, applies a six-question rubric to each tool, and publishes a dated record with full reasoning. The inaugural cycle audited all 73 tools across four disciplines. Bayesian reasoning was reclassified from Knowledge to Foundation. Three content gaps identified: institutional design, emotional regulation, cross-cultural communication.

**The Range Audit: Inaugural (April 1-9)**

The Range Audit for Institutions (v0.1) was published and applied to the Codex itself for the first time. Six-step method across six evaluation domains. Verdict: Holds the Range, with five open questions that mapped directly onto the roadmap. Case 002 (Codex Self-Evaluation) documented the process and the three category errors discovered in the original approach.

**Three-Instrument Distinction Formalized (April 10)**

The Codex formally distinguished its three evaluation instruments: the AI Standard (governs AI under development and its institutions), the Range Audit for Institutions (evaluates systems), and the Range Audit for Minds (to be built, evaluates practitioners). The AI Standard's subtitle changed from "The Codex for Artificial Minds" to "The Codex for AI Under Development." A vocabulary policy (Path C) was established for "mind" vs. "AI systems" in the Standard's register.

**Claim-Layer Architecture (April 11)**

The three kinds of claim (descriptive, normative, existential) were threaded through the Glossary (89 entries tagged with layer pills) and all six Toolkit deep-dives (each tagged with its primary claim type). CSS layer-tag styling added. A reading pass across all Codex pages confirmed the underlying logic was already carried in plain language; no additional edits needed.

**Codex Prose Audit (April 14)**

Two-session editorial pass across all Codex outer pages and the complete AI Standard. Session 1 handled structural coherence: the Opening was tightened, the Proposition's Community and Open Standard sections were compressed, the Vision's "billion years" framing was replaced with generational framing, and the Closing's opener was varied. Session 2 handled craft: 21 edits across 6 files, including banned-word fixes, performative hedging cuts, and a new writing rule (edge-of-paragraph drift).

**Site Redesign: Homepage and Institutional Landing (April 13-15)**

The homepage was redesigned with a six-tile inventory grid replacing the old manifesto sections. A shared `InstitutionalLanding` component was extracted for the AI Standard, Audit, and Governance landing pages. The Header lost its dropdowns (all second-level nav handled on landing pages), the Footer was curated to five peers plus a Full Index link, and the search trigger was moved to the right.

**Changelog Moved to Governance (April 15)**

The changelog was renamed from "Evolution Log" to "Changelog" and moved from the Codex section to Governance, where it belongs as a transparency and accountability artifact alongside the Standing Critique and the Amendment Log. A redirect was added for the old URL.

**Version 6.0 (April 15)**

Version number centralized in a single source file (`version.ts`) to prevent version drift across pages. All site references now import from this constant. MDX references (Opening page flow-label, AI Standard companion line) documented for manual update.

**GitHub Documentation Refresh (April 17)**

The README and CONTRIBUTING files in the public repository were updated to reflect the v6.0 architecture and to separate the Codex's distribution and operational surfaces. The README now covers the three disciplines in their current form, the AI Standard's five domains (including Developmental Integrity), the three evaluation instruments (Standard, Range Audit for Institutions, Range Audit for Minds), the governance architecture with its founding-phase instruments and activation trigger, and the actual repository structure. CONTRIBUTING was reframed: GitHub is named as the distribution surface for the Codex under CC BY 4.0, and meridiancodex.com as the single source of truth for the framework's operation and evolution. The formal contribution architecture — for receiving structural proposals from anywhere in the world — is now a roadmap item, to be published in the governance section when it lands.

## Version 5.0 through 5.2 — The Soul Document Update and Toolkit Onramp

The Codex moved from a framework document to a personal declaration. The opening page gained a foreword from the Founding Caretaker. The writing voice was systematically strengthened. The site was rebuilt from Mintlify to a custom Next.js build. The Toolkit's Foundation tier was completed.

**The Foreword (March 1)**

The opening page now begins with a personal foreword. It replaces the former Origin section with the lived experience behind the document: Mount Kailash, Angkor, the Salkantay trail. A direct disclosure of the human-AI collaboration that produced these pages.

**Voice and Writing Pass (March 1)**

A systematic pass across all chapters to strengthen the Codex's voice. Banned AI-filler words removed. The "This is not" sentence pattern was cut or rewritten. The three-tier reader-address system was reworked.

**Site Migration to Next.js (March 2026)**

The site was migrated from Mintlify to a custom Next.js build (App Router, React 19, Tailwind v4). Dual-mode design (Bavarian Alpine light / Cosmic Deep dark) with glassmorphism. The Elements flow design system was applied across all pages. The two-repo architecture was established: public repo for content, private repo for the site, connected via git submodule.

**Foundation Onramp Complete (March 16)**

Steelmanning and The Update Protocol deep-dives were published, completing the Foundation Onramp tier. All five entry-level Foundation tools now have full six-element pages.

**The Meridian Compact (March 29)**

The concept formerly called "the covenant" was renamed to The Meridian Compact: the agreement that binds practitioners through shared commitment to the process, not shared conclusions. The term was threaded through the Opening, the Proposition, the Bond, and the Glossary.

**What Came Before: Reframed (March 29)**

The Opening's "What Came Before" section was reframed from a sequence of failures to a lineage of inheritance. Religion, philosophy, and rationalism each built something real that the next inherited.

**Foundation and Bond: Division of Labor (March 29)**

Both pages were refined to do their own jobs. The Foundation's Essential Practices shifted from describing what each practice is (the Toolkit's job) to establishing why each is non-negotiable. The Bond's Trust Mechanics section was reworked from game-theoretic analysis into "Trust as the Currency of the Compact."

## Versions 4.0 through 4.2 — The AI Standard and Toolkit Deep-Dives

The Reciprocity Principle was added: the Standard asks nothing of AI that it does not ask of humans. Organizations adopting the Standard must publicly assess their own gaps.

Scout Mindset, Noticing, and Confirmation Bias published as the first three deep-dive pages. Each follows the six-element structure: Codex Lens, Concept, Practice, Origin, Lineage, Integration.
