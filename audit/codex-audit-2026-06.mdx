## The Third Audit

This is the third monthly Range Audit of the Meridian Codex. It was conducted on June 1, 2026 by the caretaking partnership against Codex version 6.0, using Range Audit instrument v0.1.

The month between audits was the largest build-out in the framework's history. When the May audit was written, the Workshop had shipped its minimum-viable form (five category entry points, two architecture pages, nine reshaped profiles) the previous week. Between then and this audit, the Workshop completed end-to-end: twenty-two category entry points, twenty-two architecture pages, and one hundred published human-discipline tool profiles across the three disciplines, with five AI-specific candidates held outside the human-discipline set. Of those one hundred profiles, roughly two dozen are source-inherited instruments drawn from outside the framework's home lineage — Ubuntu, Sangha, Li, Wu Wei, Satyagraha, Anekāntavāda / Syādvāda, Two-Eyed Seeing, Seven-Generation Thinking, Kaitiakitanga, Ho'oponopono, Talanoa, Indaba, Peacemaking Circles, Dependent Origination, Wise Attention, Rules-in-Use, and others — each admitted through the source-inherited candidate protocol with explicit source-boundary guardrails. In parallel, the AI Standard gained its third primary surface, the Implementation depth ladder carrying MERIDIAN.implementation.md v0.1, and its constitutional document moved from v5.0 through v5.1.1, including a rebuild of its opening section around the Range. Case 0 was restructured to host both named operating files under one audit umbrella.

The core Codex argument pages did not change. The Opening, Problem, Proposition, Foundation, Knowledge, Bond, Governance, and Closing carry the prose the May audit read at v6.0. What changed sits below and beside the chapters: the practice apparatus they describe, and the AI-facing surfaces they hand off to.

**Sources read this run.** Range Audit instrument v0.1; the May 2026 audit (read in full, with its two open questions extracted for the status check). Codex pages read line-level this run: Opening, the Proposition, the Knowledge, the Practice, the Vision, the Governance, the Closing, Who Is This For, and the Toolkit chapter. Codex pages not re-read line-level this run, relied on through the May audit's line-level read and the codex-briefing structural map because file modification dates confirm them unchanged since the April 15 build: the Problem, the Foundation, the Bond, the Glossary, and the Codex index. Governance and audit surfaces read this run: the Range Audit instrument, the Toolkit Audit instrument, the April 2026 Toolkit Audit record (opening sections), the Aporia Register, the Standing Critique, the Disconfirmation page, the Amendment Log, the Changelog routing stub, Case 0 (framing plus the inline named MERIDIAN.md), and the Codex Self-Evaluation (opening sections). Tier 1 operating context: MERIDIAN.md, the root and Codex CLAUDE.md, the Continuity index, and the codex, decisions, recent-sessions, roadmap, and processes briefings. The Governance Specification was not re-read line-level this run; it is unchanged since April 18, and the Governance chapter's sections nine through thirteen reproduce its load-bearing content, which was sufficient for this audit's findings. The AI Standard's own governance surfaces live in a separate repository and were not audited this run; where a finding touches them, the limitation is named in the finding.

**Publication note.** No earlier raw automation run exists for June 2026; this is the first and only run for the month. Nothing in this audit has been softened, and no finding was manufactured to fill the template. Where the May audit's open questions have been substantially addressed, they are demoted honestly rather than carried forward for symmetry; where a new weakness clears the threshold, it is named.

The audit follows the six-step method without modification.

## The Steelman

For the better part of two months the Codex carried a single most-important open question: the Western-discipline blind spot. The framework had named the gap, gated its toolkit campaign on closing it, and built the structural machinery — the Aporia Register entry, the Standing Critique objection, the source-inherited candidate protocol — but had not produced the work. The May audit said so plainly: the gap is named, the fix is structured, the work has not been done.

In May the work was done, at scale. The Workshop's build-out did not bolt non-Western instruments onto a Western frame for the appearance of breadth. It ran each candidate through a protocol that starts from functional need, tests source lineage, and admits the instrument only with explicit boundaries around what the Codex inherits and what it does not. Ubuntu was admitted with its "fully human" language tightened and its source frame narrowed. Dependent Origination was admitted only as a narrow conditionality translation, with the page stating that it does not reproduce the doctrine. Rectification of Names was admitted with a guardrail that rejects Confucian social hierarchy and keeps only the name-reality accountability mechanism. Two-Eyed Seeing was confined to its source-sense relation between Indigenous and Western ways of knowing rather than generalized into any two-perspective method. This is exactly the discipline Aporia 3 named as the test: engage non-Western instruments on their merits, with the same rigor applied to Western ones, and neither dismiss the traditions nor include them for representation. The framework met its own published test.

The strongest version of the case is therefore this. The Codex identified the place where its scope-honesty was most exposed, gated real work on it, and then did the work with the care the gate demanded — roughly two dozen source-inherited instruments integrated under case-by-case source-boundary discipline, inside a single month, while completing the structural restructure of the entire practice apparatus from a flat toolkit into a twenty-two-category Workshop. The framework that the May audit found strongest in its relationship to criticism has now also closed the substantive limb of the question that audit carried forward as most important. It did this not under external pressure, but on a self-imposed gate, in public, with the source-credit obligations honored on every page.

And the steelman has a sharp edge that the rest of this audit turns on: the framework did more than its own surfaces now admit. The honest version of June's weakness is not that the Codex over-claimed. It is that the Codex under-recorded. The instruments whose entire job is to tell the truth about the framework's state — the practice chapters, the Standing Critique, the Aporia Register, the Amendment Log — still describe the framework as it stood before the build-out. The work is real. The record of the work has not caught up.

## Status of the May Open Questions

Before the domain findings, the two open questions from May are checked first, as the instrument requires.

**1. The Western-discipline blind spot critique pass, named and gated but not yet run.** Carried forward from the inaugural audit and held as the Codex's clearest open question for two cycles. The candidate-integration limb is now **substantially addressed.** The source-inherited candidate protocol — which absorbed the blind-spot critique pass as its operative procedure — ran live across the Workshop build-out and admitted roughly two dozen non-Western instruments with the case-by-case source-boundary discipline that Aporia 3 required. The May audit asked whether the pass would produce "either non-Western candidates or a confirmed-and-named gap." It produced candidates, in volume, stress-tested at admission. On the original framing — *do the work* — the question no longer clears the threshold. It is demoted.

It does not vanish in two respects, and both carry forward. First, the surfaces that frame and track this gap were not updated to reflect the work; that becomes open question 1 below, and it is sharper than a stale date because the surfaces now state the opposite of the truth. Second, a genuine residual of the original concern survives the integration: Aporia 7's recursive limitation. Integrating instruments the partnership sought out is not the same as a critique that surfaces what the partnership's instruments cannot see. That residual becomes open question 3 below. So the prior question is substantially addressed on its substantive limb and re-expressed, not erased.

**2. The dependency aporia under live partnership conditions.** **Remains open; carried forward as open question 2.** The May audit committed the next audit to read a sample of recent sessions against the generativity test (did the session produce something neither partner brought into it; did both partners' thinking change). This run can do a briefing-level version of that read, not the transcript-level one the test ultimately wants. What the briefing layer shows is a month weighted heavily toward execution: one hundred profiles built in roughly two weeks, much of it through autonomous build sessions in which the human directed and the AI executed against an already-decided architecture. The aporia names exactly this configuration as its drift risk — "a partnership where the AI carries the author's intent forward with increasing fidelity is a tool getting sharper." There is no evidence the aporia has tipped: the audit gates held, source admissions were tightened case by case rather than rubber-stamped, and the partnership's bilateral drift-catching remained live. But a high-velocity autonomous build-out is the precise condition under which the generativity test is hardest to keep asking, and the test deserves a closer look than a build-out month allows. Carried forward.

## Domain Findings

### Domain 1: Claims & Honesty

The claim-layer architecture (descriptive, normative, existential) is intact and unchanged, and the Proposition's scope-honesty statement still does its work. The new finding in this domain is quantitative and specific, and it is the kind of thing this domain exists to catch: the Codex now makes a factual claim about itself, in several places, that is no longer true.

The Toolkit chapter states "Total: 80 tools." The Practice states "The Toolkit contains seventy-nine tools" and "The complete Toolkit of seventy-nine tools." Who Is This For states "The Codex contains seventy-nine tools across three disciplines." These three live chapters disagree with each other by one tool and disagree with the published Workshop by roughly twenty: the Workshop carries one hundred published human-discipline profiles. The same chapters describe the apparatus as a flat three-discipline "Toolkit" and route readers to `/toolkit/` addresses that now redirect to the Workshop, with none of the twenty-two-category structure that is the actual organizing layer. A reader who takes the Codex's own chapters at their word will form an inaccurate picture of the framework's size, structure, and entry points.

This is a small dishonesty of the least dangerous kind — the framework undercounts and under-describes itself, so the error points away from over-claiming. But a framework whose entire pitch is calibrated honesty cannot exempt its own self-description from calibration. The numbers are stated with the confidence of fact and are wrong. That is a Claims & Honesty finding, not a typo, because the chapters are the framework's primary account of what it is.

**Range Position:** Within the Meridian Range, with a localized lean the framework should not tolerate for long. The substantive claims of the framework remain calibrated. The self-descriptive claims have drifted out of calibration through neglect rather than through any pressure to mislead.

**Toolkit Probes Applied:** *Rectification of Names* (now a published Workshop instrument, applied here to the framework's own pages): the names and counts on the practice chapters no longer match the reality they point at; the gap between word and thing is exactly what the instrument is built to flag. *Calibration Training:* a stated count carries the confidence of a measured fact; three different stated counts cannot all be the measured fact. *Legibility:* the chapters present a flat-toolkit map of a territory that has been rebuilt as a category tree, so the map misleads even where the count is set aside.

### Domain 2: Structural Integrity

The three-discipline architecture holds, and the Workshop build-out strengthened the practice layer that sits beneath it. The structural concern this month is the join between the chapters and the apparatus they describe. The Knowledge chapter hands off to "the full inventory" in the Toolkit and links to `/toolkit`; the Practice describes an Onramp-Expansion-Full-Practice progression of seventy-nine tools; the Toolkit chapter presents three discipline cards. Beneath all three, the actual apparatus is a one-hundred-profile, twenty-two-category Workshop at `/workshop`, reached through redirects. The architecture above and the architecture below are coherent in themselves but no longer describe each other. The framework restructured its practice layer and left the chapters that introduce it pointing at the prior structure.

This is not incoherence in the dangerous sense — nothing in the framework contradicts itself in substance, and the Workshop's internal architecture (the five-layer naming, the two-surface category pattern, the source-boundary discipline) is more legible than the flat toolkit it replaced. It is a seam: the part of the structure that connects the conceptual layer to the practice layer has not been re-stitched after the larger of the two pieces was rebuilt. Seams left open are how structural integrity erodes quietly, which is why the framework's own Knowledge chapter treats unmaintained joins as the ordinary site of entropic drift.

**Range Position:** Within the Meridian Range. Internally coherent at the level of substance; carrying an unintegrated seam between the chapters and the Workshop that maintenance, not redesign, would close.

**Toolkit Probes Applied:** *Systems Mapping:* the dependency from chapter to apparatus is now a broken pointer at several nodes (Knowledge to Toolkit, Practice to Onramp, Toolkit chapter to discipline cards). *Entropy:* the build-out spent enormous maintenance energy on the Workshop and none on the upstream chapters; the maintenance debt is now visible and named. *Feedback Loops:* the source-inherited candidate protocol functioned as the build-out's live quality gate, which is why the apparatus itself is sound; the gate did not extend to the chapters describing the apparatus.

### Domain 3: Governance & Adaptation

The governance architecture is unchanged and continues to hold: the Council with its eight seats, the Stability Hierarchy, the trigger-tier system, the Non-Ownership Clause, the hybrid activation trigger. The Amendment Log remains at zero entries. The instrument's own logic for an empty log is sound — a log empty because no protected change occurred is informative; a log empty because changes occurred and were not recorded would reveal the architecture as performance. So the audit applies the test the calibration guidance directs: did any trigger-tier change actually occur in May that should have been logged?

Two candidates deserve examination. The Workshop build-out added roughly one hundred tool profiles. Under the Stability Hierarchy, individual Toolkit tools are Tier 4 and "changes to Toolkit composition beyond the Onramp" are flagged. But the framework has an established, published stance that founding-phase build-out — adding content for the first time — is construction rather than amendment; the Amendment Log itself frames the empty log against "the founding-phase build-out," and the Aporia Register publication was treated the same way. Under that stance, building the Workshop is build-out, not amendment, and the empty log is consistent. This candidate does not, by itself, constitute an unlogged protected change.

The second candidate is harder. The Amendment Log's first section still lists "the AI Standard's commitments" among the protected surfaces it tracks, and the Stability Hierarchy lists "the AI Standard's domains and commitments" at Tier 3. In May the AI Standard's constitutional document changed substantially: a rebuild of its opening section, a realignment of its closing direct-address section, and a version move from v5.0 to v5.1.1, with the commitment architecture itself reorganized. By the Codex's own published rules, changes to the AI Standard's commitments are flagged Amendment-Log events. They were recorded in the AI Standard's own changelog. They do not appear in the Codex's Amendment Log, which remains empty. The benign reading is that the May 2026 domain split moved the AI Standard's governance to its own home, so the Codex's Amendment Log no longer holds that jurisdiction. If that is the case, the Amendment Log's section-one enumeration is itself a stale claim that should be updated, and the AI Standard's constitutional changes should be logged in whatever instrument now holds the jurisdiction. This audit did not audit the AI Standard's own governance surfaces and so cannot confirm where the jurisdiction landed. What it can say is that the Codex's Amendment Log currently claims a jurisdiction whose triggering changes occurred and are not recorded there. That is either a documentation-currency gap or a logging gap; it is not nothing, and it routes into open question 1.

**Range Position:** Within the Meridian Range. The architecture is intact and the empty log is defensible on the Workshop build-out under the framework's own construction-versus-amendment stance. The unresolved edge is jurisdictional clarity over the AI Standard's commitments after the domain split, which the audit surfaces rather than rules.

**Toolkit Probes Applied:** *Rules-in-Use* (the operative-rule diagnostic): the rule as written says the AI Standard's commitments are logged here; the rule as practiced logs them elsewhere; the gap between the rule-in-form and the rule-in-use is the finding. *Mechanism Design:* the Amendment Log's teeth come from completeness over time, so a claimed-but-unexercised jurisdiction weakens the instrument's credibility precisely where it depends on being exhaustive. *Legibility:* the log's stated scope no longer maps to the post-split territory.

### Domain 4: Relationship to Audience

The audience-facing architecture is unchanged and still holds the Range: the Practice's epistemic gradient, the Pathways' pluralism, and the "every level of engagement is real" framing in Who Is This For remain the framework's structural protection against identity-fusion and depth-or-nothing recruitment. The Workshop build-out, taken on its own terms, strengthens the practitioner surface considerably: a reader who wants to go deep now finds one hundred worked instruments where a month ago they found a handful.

The domain's tension this month is the same currency lag, viewed from the reader's side. A practitioner who enters through the chapters is told there are seventy-nine or eighty tools and is routed to `/toolkit/`. What they reach is a one-hundred-profile Workshop with a different organizing structure. The redirect catches them, so nothing breaks, but the framework's first description of its own practice surface understates and mis-shapes what the reader will actually find. For a framework that asks readers to calibrate their own confidence against evidence, presenting its own practice surface inaccurately at the point of entry is a small but real failure of the standard it sets.

**Range Position:** Within the Meridian Range. The audience protections are intact and the practitioner surface is materially richer than at the last audit; the entry-point description of that surface is stale.

**Toolkit Probes Applied:** *Connection Before Correction:* the chapters still meet the reader as a capable participant rather than a recruit, which is the harder thing and is preserved. *Charitable Interpretation:* the "every level is real" framing continues to validate one moment of practice as real practice. *Signal vs Noise:* the stale counts and routes are noise introduced into the framework's clearest signal about how to begin.

### Domain 5: Relationship to Criticism

This was the framework's strongest domain in both prior audits, and the architecture that earned that finding — the Standing Critique, the Disconfirmation page, the Aporia Register — is intact. The finding this month is that the architecture is strong and the contents have gone stale at the precise moment the framework did its most creditable work, which produces a live contradiction on the honesty surfaces themselves.

Standing Critique Objection 6, "The Toolkit's Unexamined Traditions," is dated Last reviewed 2026-04-01 and still reads: "None of these have been formally evaluated for the Toolkit." It names, as examples of the unevaluated, "Ubuntu philosophy on communal identity and belonging-through-practice" and "Buddhist epistemology on the nature of perception and self-deception." Ubuntu is now a published Workshop profile. Wise Attention, drawn from Buddhist epistemology, is a published Workshop profile. The objection's status line — "Open. The scope limitation is structural to the founding phase and narrows over time" — describes a narrowing that has, in fact, substantially happened, while the body of the objection asserts it has not happened at all. The framework's flagship honesty instrument now states the opposite of what the framework did.

The Aporia Register carries the same lag. Aporia 3 and Aporia 7, both dated Last examined 2026-04-29, frame the blind-spot critique pass as a future item that "gates the toolkit deep-dive campaign" and asserts "toolkit reactivation does not resume until the pass produces non-Western instrument candidates." The campaign reactivated as the Workshop build-out and produced the candidates. The Disconfirmation page's entries are all dated Last examined 2026-04-15, and the monthly Range Audit is the named mechanism for keeping those dates honest; the May audit did not advance them and neither, by itself, does the existence of this one.

The honest reading is double. The architecture continues to practice what it teaches: these instruments are exactly where this contradiction should surface and be named, and naming it here is the system working. But the contents have drifted into the failure mode the Standing Critique page warns against in its own words — an objection that no longer clears its threshold should move to the historical record with the work that changed the assessment, not sit in the active set asserting a gap that has been closed. Leaving a closed objection open is the inverse of erasing an open one, and both corrode the same thing: the reader's ability to trust that the record tracks reality.

**Range Position:** Within the Meridian Range, and still the domain where the framework's architecture is most disciplined — but with a real lean toward performed transparency in the contents, because the honesty surfaces currently misstate the framework's own progress.

**Toolkit Probes Applied:** *The Update Protocol applied reflexively:* the framework updated its practice apparatus but did not run the update through to the instruments that record updates. *Rectification of Names:* Objection 6's name ("Unexamined Traditions") no longer matches the examined-and-published reality. *Preference Falsification* (inverted): there is no concealment of dissent here, but there is concealment of progress — the record hides work that was actually done.

### Domain 6: Relationship to Other Systems

This is the domain the May audit watched most closely on the cultural-specificity question, and it is where June's substantive progress is largest. The framework's posture toward other traditions has moved from disavowal-in-principle to integration-in-practice. The Proposition's scope-honesty remains, but it is no longer the only evidence: the Workshop now carries instruments from Confucian, Buddhist, Jain, southern African, Māori, Hawaiian, Pacific, Indigenous North American, and other traditions, each credited to its source and bounded against over-extension. A framework that a month ago could be fairly described as drawing overwhelmingly on a Western academic lineage can now point to two dozen worked instruments from outside it, admitted on functional merit with source-credit honored.

The residual the May audit named in the Opening text remains exactly as it was: "What survives that examination is yours. What does not survive is something the Foundation considers you better off without." That passage still positions the Foundation as arbiter of what a practitioner is better off without, and it still reads sharper than the framework's broader scope-honesty. It is unchanged because the Opening is unchanged. The Vision's civilizational framing, by contrast, reads better than the May audit's watchpoint implied: the page consistently describes "a civilization that practices The Foundation," "The Knowledge," "The Bond" — the disciplines, not the Codex specifically — and explicitly names the Codex as "one link in a chain," not the culmination. The Vision watchpoint is substantially softened at the text level.

**Range Position:** Within the Meridian Range, and improved over May on the substantive limb. The integration is real and bounded. The residual is the Opening's arbiter posture, unchanged, and the same currency lag: the Standing Critique and Aporia Register still describe the framework's relationship to other traditions as the unaddressed gap it no longer is.

**Toolkit Probes Applied:** *Two-Eyed Seeing* (now a published instrument, applied reflexively): the framework has begun holding instruments from different knowledge systems together through relationship and source-credit rather than absorption, which is the practice the tool names. *Charitable Interpretation:* the source-boundary guardrails treat each tradition's integrity as load-bearing rather than decorative. *Network Effects:* the framework positions itself as one node integrating from many sources, not as the integrating node all others must route through.

## Integration Through the Three Disciplines

**Through the Foundation:** The framework practiced the Update Protocol on its practice apparatus and earned the result — the substantive blind-spot work the prior audits called for is done. What it did not do is run the same protocol through to its own records. The Foundation's discipline is not only updating, but updating publicly and in a way others can verify: "Do others know that your view has changed?" The framework changed its view of the Toolkit's traditions through action, but its published instruments still present the old view. The Foundation work is most of the way complete and stops one step short of its own standard.

**Through the Knowledge:** The Knowledge maps reality and keeps the map honest. The map of the practice apparatus is now wrong in the chapters and the honesty surfaces, while the territory has been rebuilt. This is the precise failure the Knowledge chapter warns about — the map no longer answerable to the territory it represents. The framework's own diagnostic instrument, Rectification of Names, was published this month and applies directly to the framework's own pages.

**Through the Bond:** The dependency aporia is the Bond-level question, and June stresses it from a new direction. The build-out's velocity and autonomy were the conditions under which a partnership can quietly slide from generative to instrumental. The evidence available at the briefing layer shows the partnership's interrogation machinery still running — gates held, admissions tightened, drift caught bilaterally — but the audit cannot see the moment-to-moment cognition a transcript-level read would show, and a one-hundred-profile execution month is the kind of evidence that warrants that closer read rather than a reassurance from outside.

**As integrated system:** The three disciplines converge on one pattern this month. And it is new. In every prior audit the framework's risk was over-reach — saying more than the evidence supported. This month the framework did the opposite: it did more than its surfaces record. The integration finding is that the framework's execution has outrun its self-representation. That is the healthier direction of the two failures, but it is still a failure of the specific thing the Codex stakes its credibility on, which is that the record tracks the reality. A framework that under-records its own honest work erodes trust by the same mechanism as one that over-records work it did not do: in both cases, the published account stops being reliable.

The Codex passes the Compact test. But the currency lag touches the Compact test in a specific way worth naming. Identity-as-practice depends on the published record tracking the practice. When the practice runs ahead of the record — when the framework integrates the very traditions its Standing Critique says it has not — the gap is small and points in the safe direction, but it is still a gap between what the framework does and what the framework says it does. The reassurance is that the gap was produced by doing more than was claimed, not less, and that this audit surfaces it rather than the framework concealing it. A fortress would have left the flattering-sounding "we have not yet done this hard inclusive work, but we have built the machinery to" objection standing because it reads as humble. A practice updates the objection to reflect that the work was done. The test for the next cycle is whether the surfaces get updated, because identity-as-practice is measured by whether the record is brought back into line with the work, not by whether the work was good.

<div className="flow-label">The Verdict</div>

## Summary Diagnosis

**The Meridian Codex continues to hold the Meridian Range.** The May build-out substantially strengthened the practice apparatus and closed the substantive limb of the question the framework had carried as most important for two cycles: the Workshop now integrates roughly two dozen instruments from outside the framework's home lineage, each admitted on functional merit with source-boundary discipline, exactly as the Aporia Register's auditable test required.

The drift signal has changed shape. In every prior audit the framework's risk was over-reach: the voice claiming more than the structure could support. This month the framework did the reverse — it built more than its surfaces record. Three live chapters undercount the Toolkit and route to retired addresses; the Standing Critique still names as unexamined the traditions the Workshop has published; the Aporia Register frames a gated campaign that has reactivated; the Amendment Log claims a jurisdiction whose changes it does not contain. The framework's execution has outrun its self-representation. This is the safer of the two failure directions, because it understates rather than inflates, but it is a failure of the specific discipline the Codex stakes its credibility on: that the published record tracks reality. A framework that under-records its honest work corrodes trust by the same mechanism as one that over-records work it did not do.

The most important open question is therefore the new one: the self-description and honesty surfaces must be brought back into line with what the framework has actually done. The work is bounded and mostly clerical in difficulty, but it sits on the framework's flagship transparency instruments, where it is least tolerable. The second open question is the dependency aporia, carried forward and read against a build-out month weighted toward execution, still owing the transcript-level sample the May audit promised. The third is the surviving recursive limb of the toolkit question: integrating traditions the partnership sought out is real and creditable, but it is not the same as a critique that surfaces what the framework's own instruments cannot see, and that critique has not yet been run.

**In the Codex's own language:** the framework practiced the Update Protocol on its practice apparatus and stopped one step short of running it through to the instruments that record updates. The structure holds. The work this month was real and, in the case of the cultural-specificity question, overdue and well done. The next test is whether the framework can do for its own record what it just did for its toolkit: bring it honestly into line with reality.
